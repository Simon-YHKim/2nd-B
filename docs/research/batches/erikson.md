**Note**: Erikson's primary works (*Childhood and Society* 1950; *Identity: Youth and Crisis* 1968; *The Life Cycle Completed* 1982) are books without single-paper DOIs. Marcia operationalized the identity stage, Vaillant tested the model longitudinally, and McAdams operationalized generativity (Stage 7).

## Age Range Coverage

Erikson's 8 stages span the entire lifespan; each crisis is the central organizing theme for that period.

- **Child (0–12)** → applicable: Stages 1–4
  - Trust vs Mistrust (0–1.5)
  - Autonomy vs Shame/Doubt (1.5–3)
  - Initiative vs Guilt (3–5)
  - Industry vs Inferiority (5–12)
- **Adolescent (13–17)** → applicable: Stage 5 — Identity vs Role Confusion
- **Young Adult (18–29)** → applicable: Stage 6 — Intimacy vs Isolation; identity carries over (Marcia)
- **Adult (30–49)** → applicable: Stage 7 — Generativity vs Stagnation (early phase)
- **Midlife (50–64)** → applicable: Stage 7 (deepening)
- **Elderly (65+)** → applicable: Stage 8 — Integrity vs Despair

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated)

Stage-specific prompts. The Advisor selects based on user's stated age range. **Reflection prompts, not diagnostic items.**

**Korean** (stage-aware)
- (청소년/청년) 지금 본인이 ''누구다''라고 한 문장으로 말한다면 어떻게 말씀하시겠어요? 그 답이 작년과 비교해 얼마나 달라졌나요? (Identity)
- (20–30대) 가까운 사람에게 진짜 본인을 드러내는 게 어려웠던 최근 일이 있나요? 어떤 부분이 가장 망설여졌어요? (Intimacy)
- (30–60대) 본인이 떠난 뒤에도 남길 만한 것이 있다면 그게 무엇이길 바라시나요? (Generativity)
- (60대+) 지금까지 살아오신 삶을 되돌아보실 때 가장 ''잘 살았다''고 느끼는 시기는 언제이고 왜 그렇게 느끼세요? (Integrity)

**English** (stage-aware)
- (adolescent/young adult) If you had to say in one sentence "who I am," what would it be? How is that different from a year ago? (Identity)
- (20s–30s) When was the last time it was hard to be your real self with someone close? What part felt riskiest? (Intimacy)
- (30s–60s) If something of yours outlasted you, what would you want it to be? (Generativity)
- (60s+) Looking back, what's the stretch of your life that feels most "well-lived"? What made it that way? (Integrity)

### Trait Extraction Cues

- **Identity (adolescent → young adult)**: explicit "who am I" questioning, role experimentation, foreclosure vs moratorium vs achievement language (Marcia 1966); identity-status shifts across entries.
- **Intimacy (young adult)**: depth of self-disclosure described, fear-of-rejection vs commitment ambivalence, distinction between connection and merger.
- **Generativity (adult, midlife)**: language of mentoring, legacy, creating, caring for next generation (McAdams & de St. Aubin 1992 — LGS subscales: passing knowledge, caring, leaving legacy, community contribution, creativity).
- **Integrity (elderly)**: life-review language, acceptance vs regret patterns, narrative coherence across past chapters.

### Advisor Guidance Patterns

- Match prompt depth to the user's current life stage; do not push generativity questions on teens or identity-status questions on retirees.
- Use Mitchell et al. (2021) finding: low identity resolution in emerging adulthood is not fate — those individuals showed faster growth in intimacy/generativity/integrity later, nearly converging by their 60s. Use this for hope-anchored guidance in users who report "I still don't know who I am" in their 30s.
- For Korean users, reference Park et al. (2023): identity development in Korean young adults is influenced by both individualist and collectivist demands. Avoid pushing pure Western "discover your unique self" framing; include relational identity prompts.
- Never use stages prescriptively ("you should be working on intimacy now") — use them descriptively ("a common challenge at this stage is X — does it land for you?").

## Cautions & Limitations

- **Stage theory is heuristic, not deterministic**: people revisit earlier stages, work on multiple stages simultaneously, and timelines vary widely. Modern lifespan researchers treat stages as overlapping themes, not gates.
- **Western, male, mid-20th-century origin**: Erikson developed the model in a specific cultural moment; stages map imperfectly onto contemporary, non-Western, non-binary, or LGBTQ+ trajectories. Marcia's identity statuses were originally validated on college men (Marcia 1966).
- **Korean cultural caveat**: Park et al. (2023) note Korean young-adult identity formation blends individualist exploration with collectivist relational obligations; pure "exploration vs commitment" framing under-represents this. Use Korean-validated process models alongside Marcia.
- **Generativity ≠ parenting**: Generativity includes mentoring, creating, civic contribution. Do not assume childless users score low (McAdams' LGS subscales explicitly include non-parental forms).
- **Integrity vs despair is not "happy old age vs sad old age"**: it is the integration of one's life narrative. A user with grief or regret can still be in integrity if they hold it coherently.
- **Not clinical**: Erikson stages do not diagnose. Identity diffusion is not "identity disorder" — it is a developmental state.
