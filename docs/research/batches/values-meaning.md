# Framework: Values, Meaning & Self-Concordant Goals (the "philosophy" layer)

> Batch produced via `docs/research/psychology-handoff.md`. All DOIs verified against Crossref / publisher record (May 2026).
>
> This batch directly addresses **"나의 철학을 발굴하는 방법"** — how a person comes to articulate what they actually value, what gives their life meaning, and which goals are genuinely theirs (vs. inherited from family/culture/should). Companion to `self-knowledge.md` (the *process* of self-discovery) and `assessment-landscape.md` (Schwartz Values is cited there as the *taxonomy*).

## The Central Construct — Self-Concordance

Sheldon & Elliot (1999) is the single most operationally useful finding for the "is this actually my goal?" question 2nd-Brain users repeatedly arrive at:

| Goal type | "Why am I doing this?" | Wellbeing outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Intrinsic** | "Because I genuinely enjoy / find it meaningful" | Strongest |
| **Identified** | "Because I see it as important to who I am" | Strong |
| **Introjected** | "Because I'd feel guilty / ashamed if I didn't" | Weak / negative |
| **External** | "Because someone is making me / it's expected" | Weak / negative |

Self-concordance = (Intrinsic + Identified) − (Introjected + External). Sheldon & Elliot's longitudinal finding: self-concordant goals are **more likely to be attained AND produce more wellbeing when attained**. Goals pursued for introjected/external reasons can be achieved but produce little durable wellbeing.

This directly maps to SDT (already in seed) at the goal level rather than the basic-need level.

- **Adolescent (13–17)**: applicable — MLQ validated in adolescent samples internationally.

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated)

**Korean — values surface (Wilson VLQ 10 domains)**
- 본인 인생에서 ''중요하다''고 느끼는 영역을 10개 정도 떠올린다면 — 가족, 친밀한 관계, 양육, 친구, 일/직업, 교육/학습, 여가, 영성, 시민/공동체, 건강 — 그 중 본인이 ''여기에 정말 신경 쓴다''고 느끼는 영역과, ''신경 쓰지만 실제로 시간을 들이지 못하는'' 영역은 각각 어떤 거예요? (Importance × Action gap)
- 본인이 늘 ''언젠가는…''이라고 말하지만 정작 시작하지 못한 게 있다면 어떤 거예요? 무엇이 막고 있어요? (Value-action gap)

**Korean — self-concordance (Sheldon & Elliot 1999)**
- 지금 본인이 가장 큰 노력을 들이고 있는 목표가 있다면, 그게 ''내가 정말 원해서''(intrinsic/identified)인가요, ''안 하면 죄책감/부담감이 들어서''(introjected) 또는 ''누가 시켜서''(external)인가요?
- 만약 아무도 본인을 평가하지 않는다면 본인은 그 일을 똑같이 하시겠어요?

**Korean — meaning (Steger MLQ 2 dimensions)**
- 본인은 지금 인생에서 ''의미를 찾고 있는 중'' 같으세요, 아니면 ''의미를 이미 찾았다'' 같으세요? (Search vs Presence)
- 본인의 삶이 ''왜 살 가치가 있는가''라는 질문에 한 문장으로 답한다면?

**Korean — best possible self (King 2001)**
- 5년 후, 본인이 가장 잘 풀린 시나리오에서 어떤 하루를 보내고 계실까요? 아침에 일어나서 어떻게 하루가 흘러갈 것 같아요? (Best possible self — 20분 글쓰기 프롬프트의 변주)

**English — values surface**
- Of the life domains that matter — family, intimate relationships, parenting, friends, work, education, leisure, spirituality, community, health — which ones do you genuinely care about, and which ones do you care about but never make time for? (Importance × Action gap)
- What's something you keep saying "someday" about? What's stopping you? (Value-action gap)

**English — self-concordance**
- The goal you're putting the most effort into right now — is it because you actually want it, because you'd feel guilty if you didn't, or because someone expects it? (Self-concordance type)
- If no one were judging you, would you still do this?

**English — meaning**
- Are you currently *searching for* meaning, or do you feel you've *found* it? (Steger Search vs Presence)
- If you had to answer "why is life worth living" in one sentence, what would you say?

**English — best possible self**
- Five years from now, in the best plausible scenario, walk me through one day. What would the morning look like? (King 2001 best-possible-self adaptation)

### Trait Extraction Cues

- **Self-concordant goals** (Sheldon & Elliot 1999): "I want to" / "I chose to" framing, intrinsic enjoyment descriptors, value-aligned reasoning when explaining goals.
- **Introjected goals** (concerning signal): "I should" / "I have to" / guilt-anticipation framing, persistent goals the user does not seem to enjoy.
- **External goals**: explicit external attribution ("my parents want me to", "the company expects").
- **Value-action gap**: stated importance of a domain that gets zero or near-zero entry-time across weeks/months.
- **Meaning presence** (Steger MLQ): explicit purpose statements, integration of past-present-future, value-articulation that persists across entries.
- **Meaning search** (Steger MLQ): question-form entries about purpose/why, exploration language, openness to revising current path.
- **Best-possible-self engagement** (King 2001): vivid future-positive imagery that's specific to the user (not generic).

### Advisor Guidance Patterns

- **Surface introjected goals carefully**: Sheldon & Elliot (1999) show these are real wellbeing risks, but the user often doesn't recognize them. The Advisor can mirror: "you've described this goal three times this month — each time the reason was about avoiding guilt. Was that the original reason you started?" Reflective, not directive.
- **Use King (2001) best-possible-self writing as a periodic ritual**: 20 minutes, 4 days in succession, was the original protocol; for 2nd-Brain a monthly best-possible-self prompt is a plausible adaptation. King's finding: this is *less upsetting than trauma writing* and still produces wellbeing gains — gentler entry point.
- **Distinguish meaning-search from meaning-absence**: Steger et al. (2006) show that *searching for* meaning is associated with lower wellbeing in Western adults but with *higher* wellbeing in some Eastern samples. Do not pathologize an actively-searching user.
- **Use the importance-vs-action grid** (VLQ-inspired): track stated value-domain importance over time, and contrast with where the user actually spends entry-time (proxy for attention). Persistent gaps are the most actionable Advisor signal.
- **For Korean users**: K-MIL-CQ (Choi & Shin 2022) is validated for elementary children but adult Korean meaning-in-life instruments without DOI exist. Use Korean cultural framing of meaning — relational/collective contribution often weighted equally with personal purpose; do not impose Western individual-purpose framing.
- **Do not present values as fixed**: values evolve across life stages (see `erikson.md` and `soc-successful-aging.md`). The Advisor should periodically re-elicit, not store as final.

## Cautions & Limitations

- **Values-clarification fad risk**: pop self-help reduces values to "find your one true purpose," which the science does not support. Values are domain-multiple, contextually-shifting, and evolve. Avoid singular-purpose framing.
- **Best-possible-self interventions are not therapy**: King (2001) effect sizes are modest; do not promise transformation. Some users may find the prompt aversive (low optimism, current depression) — the Frattaroli (2006) moderators in `self-knowledge.md` apply here too.
- **Self-concordance assumes choice space**: a user with limited material options (financial dependence, caregiving obligation) cannot freely re-choose. Advisor should name structural constraints, not push individual "choose differently" framing.
- **Cultural variance in meaning search**: searching for meaning correlates differently with wellbeing across cultures (Steger and follow-up work). Korean and East Asian search-state should not be treated as deficient.
- **Korean adult meaning measure unconfirmed**: no DOI-registered Korean adult MLQ validation was confirmable in this session; the K-MIL-CQ is child-only. Future batch should add a verified adult Korean meaning measure.
- **Not philosophical counseling**: this batch supports articulating personal values, not resolving existential questions. Route deep meaning-crisis distress to professional support.
