## Age Range Coverage

- **Child (0–12)**: partially — Big Five emerges by middle childhood; parent-report and child-report adaptations exist (e.g., BFQ-C) but structure not yet fully consolidated.
- **Adolescent (13–17)**: applicable — BFI/BFI-2 validated in adolescent samples; 5-factor structure replicates.
- **Young Adult (18–29)**: applicable — largest evidence base; period of greatest mean-level change (Conscientiousness ↑, Negative Emotionality ↓).
- **Adult (30–49)**: applicable — continued maturation; rank-order stability high.
- **Midlife (50–64)**: applicable — relative stability with modest decline in Openness in late midlife.
- **Elderly (65+)**: applicable — Korean BFI-K validated in elderly samples; small declines in Extraversion and Openness reported.

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated)

Items below are inspired by BFI-2 facet structure (Soto & John 2017) but rephrased as open-ended reflection prompts. **Do not present as a diagnostic test.**

**Korean**
- 처음 만나는 사람들이 많은 자리에서 평소 본인은 어떤 모습이신가요? (Extraversion)
- 마감이나 일정이 정해져 있을 때 그게 도움이 되시나요, 아니면 부담이 되시나요? (Conscientiousness)
- 누군가 본인 의견에 강하게 반대했던 최근 일이 있다면 그때 어떻게 반응하셨나요? (Agreeableness)
- 일이 잘 안 풀렸을 때 그 감정이 보통 며칠 가시나요, 아니면 금방 회복되시나요? (Negative Emotionality, reversed)
- 최근에 익숙하지 않은 아이디어나 경험에 끌렸던 적이 있다면 어떤 거였나요? (Open-Mindedness)

**English**
- When you walk into a room of strangers, what's your default — start conversations or hang back? (Extraversion)
- Tell me about a recent deadline. Did the structure help you, or weigh on you? (Conscientiousness)
- Last time someone disagreed with you on something that mattered, what happened? (Agreeableness)
- When things go wrong, how long does it usually take you to bounce back? (Negative Emotionality, reversed)
- What's an unfamiliar idea or experience that's caught your attention recently? (Open-Mindedness)

### Trait Extraction Cues

From journal entries, map language patterns to BFI-2 domains:

- **Extraversion**: frequency of social activity mentions, energy descriptors ("charged up" / "drained"), initiation vs reaction in social events.
- **Agreeableness**: how conflicts are framed (perspective-taking vs grievance), pronoun ratio ("we" vs "I"), concern for others' feelings.
- **Conscientiousness**: planning vocabulary (lists, schedules), follow-through on stated intentions, response to disorder.
- **Negative Emotionality**: valence of emotion words, rumination markers (revisiting the same event across entries), recovery time.
- **Open-Mindedness**: variety of topics, aesthetic/curiosity vocabulary, willingness to revise previously stated views.

**Rule**: Do not classify on a single entry. Wait for consistent patterns across ≥10 entries before tagging.

### Advisor Guidance Patterns

- Use trait estimates as **soft priors**, never as labels. Phrase: "Your entries often touch on X — does that match how you see yourself?"
- For change-oriented guidance, anchor on Bleidorn et al. (2022): mean-level trait change is normative across adulthood; small shifts are realistic, dramatic personality reinvention is not.
- Match guidance to trait pattern:
  - High Negative Emotionality → recovery-time strategies (sleep, exposure-titration), not "stop worrying"
  - Low Conscientiousness → environmental scaffolding (defaults, friction reduction) before willpower
  - High Openness → reflective-essay prompts; low Openness → structured checklists
- **Never frame any trait as "good" or "bad"** — every facet has tradeoffs (Soto & John 2017).

## Cautions & Limitations

- **Self-report bias**: BFI is self-report; observer reports diverge meaningfully (McCrae & Costa 1987). Treat journal-derived trait inferences as a partial signal.
- **Not diagnostic**: Big Five is not a clinical instrument. Do **not** flag depression based on high Negative Emotionality alone.
- **Cross-cultural caveat**: the 5-factor structure replicates broadly, but facet-level correlations vary. Korean samples sometimes show different relations between Agreeableness facets and collectivist values (Choi et al. 2025). Reference Korean-validated measures, not direct translation.
- **Change is slow**: mean-level changes in adulthood are real but modest (~0.1–0.2 SD per decade). Avoid promising "personality transformation."
- **Children under 12**: trait structure not fully consolidated; use developmentally appropriate frameworks (Erikson, attachment) instead.
- **Not for crisis use**: this framework does not detect or treat mental-health crises. Routing belongs to the Safety Classifier.
