# Framework: Interpersonal Relationships (Circumplex + Gottman Research)

> Batch produced via `docs/research/psychology-handoff.md`. All DOIs verified against Crossref / publisher record (May 2026).
>
> This batch covers **adult interpersonal functioning** — how people approach, navigate, and damage close relationships. Anchored on Wiggins's interpersonal circumplex (IPC) as the structural model and Gottman's longitudinal couples research as the behavioral evidence base. Attachment is the developmental upstream (already in `attachment.md`); this batch is the **proximal behavior** layer.

## Age Range Coverage

- **Child (0–12)**: not applicable — IPC and Gottman work target adolescent+ social cognition.
- **Adolescent (13–17)**: applicable — IPC dimensions emerge; peer-relationship research domain.
- **Young Adult (18–29)**: applicable — IPC and IIP-32 validated; early Gottman applies to dating relationships.
- **Adult (30–49)**: applicable — peak research window for Gottman couples work and Korean marital studies.
- **Midlife (50–64)**: applicable — Yoo (2022) Korean sample spans adult lifespan.
- **Elderly (65+)**: applicable — interpersonal needs and patterns persist; less specific research in this batch.

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated)

Anchored on IPC's two axes — **agency** (dominant ↔ submissive) and **communion** (warm ↔ cold) — and Gottman's "Four Horsemen" risk markers (criticism, defensiveness, contempt, stonewalling). **Reflection prompts only.**

**Korean**
- 가까운 사람과 갈등이 생겼을 때 본인은 보통 어떻게 반응하세요 — 먼저 말을 꺼내는 편이세요, 아니면 시간이 지나길 기다리시는 편이세요? (Agency dimension)
- 누군가에게 ''고맙다''고 표현하는 게 본인에게 쉬운 일인가요, 어려운 일인가요? (Communion dimension)
- 최근에 본인이 누군가에게 ''너 왜 그래''라는 말을 한 적이 있다면 어떤 상황이었어요? (Criticism marker — Gottman)
- 상대가 본인에게 비난할 때 본인은 보통 ''아닌데, 왜냐하면…''으로 시작하시나요? (Defensiveness marker)
- 본인이 가장 자주 빠지는 인간관계 패턴이 있다면 어떤 거예요 — 너무 가까워지거나, 너무 멀어지거나, 한쪽으로 기울거나? (Interpersonal complementarity)

**English**
- When you're in conflict with someone close, what's your default — bring it up first, or wait for it to pass? (Agency)
- How easy is it for you to say "thank you" or express gratitude? (Communion)
- When was the last time you said "what's wrong with you" or "why do you always…" to someone? (Criticism marker — Gottman)
- When someone criticizes you, do you tend to start with "no, because…"? (Defensiveness marker)
- What's the interpersonal pattern you fall into most — getting too close, too distant, or imbalanced? (Interpersonal complementarity)

### Trait Extraction Cues

**IPC dimensions** (Wiggins 1979):
- **Agentic-dominant**: directing language, taking-charge narratives, low tolerance for being directed.
- **Agentic-submissive**: deferring language, "I just went along with it", avoidance of asserting.
- **Communal-warm**: affection language, named others, mutual-care descriptions.
- **Communal-cold**: distance language, transactional framing of others, low named-other density.

**Gottman risk markers** (from Gottman & Levenson 2000 and later corpus):
- **Criticism**: attacks on character, not behavior ("you're so selfish" vs "you didn't call when you said you would").
- **Defensiveness**: refusing responsibility, counter-attacks, victim posture.
- **Contempt**: sarcasm, name-calling, eye-rolling described — the single strongest predictor of divorce.
- **Stonewalling**: withdrawal, silent treatment, shutting down.

**IIP-32 problem patterns** (Bailey et al. 2018; Horowitz et al. 2000):
- Domineering / Intrusive / Overly Nurturant / Overly Accommodating / Non-assertive / Socially Avoidant / Cold / Vindictive — eight octants around the IPC.

**Korean cultural markers** (Yoo 2022):
- Gender role attitude (traditional vs egalitarian) is a strong moderator of communication quality and satisfaction in Korean adult samples — do not interpret communication patterns without context on role expectations the user is operating under.

### Advisor Guidance Patterns

- Use IPC as the **structural map** for interpersonal patterns — agency and communion are nearly universal axes across cultures, more robust than type systems.
- For users showing Gottman risk markers in journal entries, name the pattern descriptively, not pathologically: "you wrote about 'why does she always' three times this week — that framing is one Gottman's research links to relationship strain. Useful to track?"
- **Never** infer relationship quality from one entry or one person's account. Gottman's predictive power comes from **observed dyadic interaction**, not self-report alone.
- For Korean users with marital/family content, surface Yoo (2022)'s finding that role-expectation gaps (traditional vs egalitarian) explain a substantial share of dissatisfaction. Pose this as a context check, not a diagnosis.
- Complement attachment (developmental) with IPC (proximal behavior): a user with avoidant attachment may show "cold" or "socially avoidant" IPC patterns; surface both layers as related but distinct.
- **Do not prescribe communication scripts** ("use I-statements"). These are pop-therapy heuristics, not validated across cultures. Reflection > scripting.

## Cautions & Limitations

- **Self-report ≠ dyadic observation**: Gottman's predictive validity is grounded in coded interactions, not journals. Journal-derived inferences are partial signals.
- **Cultural variance of relational warmth**: Communion expression differs by culture — Korean relational warmth often centers on indirect gestures (food, presence, sustained obligation) rather than explicit verbal affection. IPC vocabulary should be culturally adapted.
- **Drama triangle, attachment styles popular usage**: many users describe relationships via Karpman's drama triangle ("I keep being the rescuer") or pop attachment labels. These are useful self-narratives but not psychometric assessments; treat as the user's self-construct, not as inference.
- **Korean marriage/family research is gendered and heteronormative**: most Korean marital satisfaction work (including Yoo 2022) studies heterosexual couples. Application to other relationship structures requires explicit translation.
- **Trauma and abuse**: interpersonal patterns rooted in trauma require professional support. Route distress signals through Safety Classifier rather than processing in-product.
- **Not couples therapy**: 2nd-Brain is single-user; couples-level dynamics inferable from one partner's journal are inherently partial. Never make claims about the absent party.
- **No diagnostic claims**: IPC quadrants and Gottman risk markers describe patterns, not disorders. Do not pathologize.
