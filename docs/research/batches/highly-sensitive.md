## Foundational Sources

1. Aron, E. N., & Aron, A. (1997). Sensory-processing sensitivity and its relation to introversion and emotionality. *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 73*(2), 345–368. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.73.2.345 — *Tier A.* SPS is a distinct trait (deeper processing, stronger reactivity), separable from introversion and emotionality; origin of the HSP scale.

## Recent Validation (last 10 years)

1. Lionetti, F., Aron, A., Aron, E. N., Burns, G. L., Jagiellowicz, J., & Pluess, M. (2018). Dandelions, tulips and orchids: Evidence for the existence of low-sensitive, medium-sensitive and high-sensitive individuals. *Translational Psychiatry, 8*, 24. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41398-017-0090-6 — *Tier A.* Three sensitivity groups (orchids 31% high, tulips medium, dandelions 29% low); sensitivity is a continuum, most people in the middle.
2. Greven, C. U., Lionetti, F., Booth, C., Aron, E. N., Fox, E., … Pluess, M. (2019). Sensory processing sensitivity in the context of Environmental Sensitivity: A critical review and development of research agenda. *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews, 98*, 287–305. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2019.01.009 — *Tier A.* Vantage sensitivity: high sensitivity also means benefiting more from positive environments — a double-edged trait, not just vulnerability.

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated framing)

**Korean**
- 다른 사람보다 자극(소리·분위기·감정)에 더 크게 반응한다고 느낀 적은요?
- 어떤 환경에서 ''살아난다''고 느끼고, 어떤 환경이 빨리 지치게 하나요?

**English**
- Do you notice you react more strongly to stimuli (noise, mood, emotion) than others?
- Which environments make you come alive, and which drain you fast?

### Trait Extraction Cues
- Distinguish high-reactivity (a trait) from distress (a state) — same report,
  different meaning. Look for the deeper-processing signature, not just upset.

### Advisor Guidance Patterns
- Frame sensitivity as a degree on a continuum, never as a label or a flaw.
- Surface vantage sensitivity (Greven 2019): which environments let the user thrive.
- Never imply a condition to fix; mirror as temperament self-knowledge.

## Cautions & Limitations

- Not a clinical diagnosis; never frame as a disorder or something to treat.
- The HSP construct overlaps with neuroticism — keep it about processing depth, not distress.
- If sensitivity reports co-occur with crisis markers, route to `crisis-detection` first.

## Cross-references

- `big-five` — overlap/distinction with neuroticism + introversion.
- `self-compassion` — counters the "something's wrong with me" framing.
- `self-knowledge` — separates trait reactivity from rumination.
