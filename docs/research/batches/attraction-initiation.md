## Foundational Sources

1. Aron, A., Melinat, E., Aron, E. N., Vallone, R. D., & Bator, R. J. (1997). The experimental generation of interpersonal closeness: A procedure and some preliminary findings. *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin, 23*(4), 363–377. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/0146167297234003 — *Tier A.* Escalating reciprocal self-disclosure generates closeness even between strangers (the "36 questions"); intimacy is built, not found.

## Recent Validation (last 15 years)

1. Finkel, E. J., Eastwick, P. W., Karney, B. R., Reis, H. T., & Sprecher, S. (2012). Online dating: A critical analysis from the perspective of psychological science. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest, 13*(1), 3–66. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/1529100612436522 — *Tier A.* Profiles and matching algorithms predict little about real compatibility; attraction emerges from interaction, not selection.
2. Montoya, R. M., & Horton, R. S. (2013). A meta-analytic investigation of the processes underlying the similarity-attraction effect. *Journal of Social and Personal Relationships, 30*(1), 64–94. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/0265407512452989 — *Tier A.* Perceived similarity drives attraction more than actual similarity; initial attraction ≠ deep compatibility.

## Application to 2nd-Brain

### Interview Question Examples (validated framing)

**Korean**
- 누군가에게 끌릴 때, 처음 마음이 움직이는 건 보통 무엇 때문인가요?
- 관계가 더 가까워지려면 먼저 한 걸음 여는 쪽인가요, 기다리는 쪽인가요?

**English**
- When you're drawn to someone, what usually moves you first?
- In getting closer, do you tend to open up first or wait?

### Trait Extraction Cues
- Selection-framing vs interaction-framing: does the user describe finding "the
  right one" (browsing mindset) or building with someone (interaction mindset)?
- Note what the user reads as "similarity" — perceived overlap often ≠ actual.

### Advisor Guidance Patterns
- Reframe from "how to be picked / how to be attractive" to "how do I tend to
  start and deepen connection" — the only on-mission angle.
- Surface the build-not-find principle (Aron 1997): one small step of opening up.
- Never give pickup/seduction tactics; this is self-understanding, not coaching.

## Cautions & Limitations

- Off-mission risk: the pop-psych "attraction" genre is mostly weak/manipulative.
  Cite only the perceived-similarity + self-disclosure + interaction findings.
- Minors: keep age-appropriate; defer to C10 consent tiers.
- Never advise on someone else's behavior — only the user's own patterns.
